## Add these core conditions

### Trend template

Use these trend filters:
- Close above 50 EMA.
- 50 EMA above 150 EMA.
- 150 EMA above 200 EMA.
- Close above 200 EMA or at least above a rising 150 EMA.
- Price near 52-week high, not near 52-week low. [scribd](https://www.scribd.com/document/352063047/The-Trend-Template-Mark-Minervini)

This removes weak charts and makes sure you are only looking at stocks in a healthy stage-2 type trend. [chartink](https://chartink.com/screener/volatility-compression)

### Compression rules

Add these volatility filters:
- ATR(14) lower than ATR(14) 10 days ago.
- ATR(14) divided by close below a strict threshold.
- Daily candles getting smaller.
- At least 2 to 4 pullbacks, each one shallower than the last. [finermarketpoints](https://www.finermarketpoints.com/post/what-is-a-vcp-pattern-mark-minervini-s-volatility-contraction-pattern-explained)

This is the real VCP heart: volatility should shrink progressively, not just be low on one random day. [arongroups](https://arongroups.co/technical-analyze/vcp-pattern-strategy/)

### Volume rules

Add these volume filters:
- Volume drying up during the base.
- Current volume below 20-day average during consolidation.
- Breakout day volume at least 1.5x to 2x average volume.
- Prefer stocks with accumulation signs, not repeated heavy selloff days. [sharpely](https://sharpely.in/blogs/volatility-contraction-pattern-vcp-rule-based-screener-built-high-quality/)

Volume dry-up tells you supply is disappearing, while breakout volume tells you demand is returning with strength. [finermarketpoints](https://www.finermarketpoints.com/post/what-is-a-vcp-pattern-mark-minervini-s-volatility-contraction-pattern-explained)

### Leadership rules

Add these filters:
- Relative strength strong.
- Stock near 52-week high.
- Sector/industry strength positive.
- Earnings and sales growth healthy.
- Avoid illiquid or thin names. [finermarketpoints](https://www.finermarketpoints.com/post/mark-minervini-vcp-patterns-why-sector-and-thematic-filters-matter-more-than-stock-selection)

This is important because a perfect-looking chart in a weak stock often fails, while a strong leader in a strong group can break out cleanly. [trendspider](https://trendspider.com/trading-tools-store/collection/minervini-trend-template-vcp/)

## Clean version for Chartink-style scan

Here is the **ideal logic** in plain language:

- Daily Close > 50 EMA.
- 50 EMA > 150 EMA.
- 150 EMA > 200 EMA.
- Close near 52-week high.
- ATR(14) falling versus 10 days ago.
- ATR(14) / Close very low.

If your goal is **accuracy over quantity**, use the scan in two stages:
